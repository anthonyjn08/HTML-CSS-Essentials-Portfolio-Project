Career Highlights and Achievements

Here is a list of Kobe Bryant's achievements throughout his proffesional career and some of his career highlights alongside some videos of those highlights.

Career Achievements

  • 5x NBA Champion - 2000, 2001, 2002, 2009, 2010
  • 2x NBA Finals MVP - 2009, 2010
  • NBA regular season MVP - 2008
  • 2x NBA Scoring Champion - 2006, 2007
  • 18x NBA All-Star 1998, 2000-2016
  • 4x NBA All-Star MVP - 2002, 2007, 2009, 2011
  • 11x All-NBA First Team - 2002-2004, 2006-2013
  • 2x All-NBA Second Team - 2000, 2001
  • 2x All-NBA Third Team - 1999, 2005
  • 9x NBA All-Defensive First Team - 2000, 2003, 2004, 2006-2011
  • 3x NBA All-Defensive Second Team - 2001, 2002, 2012
  • NBA Slam Dunk Contest champion - 1997
  • NBA All-Rookie Second Team - 1997
  • 2x Olympic Gold Medalist - 2008, 2012
  • FIBA Amercias Championship Gold Medalist - 2007

Career Highlights

  • 1996 Bryant is selected with the 13th pick in the NBA Draft by the Charlotte Hornets and is traded to the LA Lakers for Vlade Divac.
  • 1997 In his 1st All Star Weekend Bryant competes in the Rookie Challenge and also wins his 1st Dunk Contest, becoming the youngest ever winner in the process.
  • 2000-2002 Bryant's LA Lakers win 3 consecutive NBA Championships.
  • 2002 Bryant scores a then career high 56 points vs the Memphis Grizzlies.
  • 2002 Bryant becomes the youngest player to win 3 NBA Championships.
  • 2003 Bryant sets a then NBA record for most 3 pointers in a game, making 12 vs the Seatle Supersonics.
  • 2003 Bryant scores 40 or more points for 9 consecutive games.
  • 2005 Bryant scores another then career high 62 points in just 3 quarters vs the Dallas Mavericks.
  • 2006 Bryant scores a career high 81 points in a 122-104 victory vs the Toronto Raptors, the 2nd highest individual points total in an NBA game.
  • 2006 Before the start of the 2006-2007 season Bryant changes his jersey number from 8 to 24, his original high school number and the number he had originally wanted upon entering the league, but was unavailable at that time.
  • 2007 Bryant becomes only second player in NBA history with four consectutive 50 point games.
  • 2007 Bryant sets a then NBA record becoming the youngest player to score 20,000 points at age 29 and 122 days.
  • 2008 Bryant is named the NBA regular season MVP.
  • 2009 Bryant scores 61 points to set a then NBA record for most points at Madison Square Garden.
  • 2009 Bryant wins his fourth NBA Championship and first NBA Finals MVP award.
  • 2009 Bryant becomes the youngest player to 25,000 points at age 31 years and 151 days, surpassing Wilt Chamberlain.
  • 2010 Bryant leads the Lakers past the Boston Celtics in 7 games to win his 5th and final NBA Championship and second Finals MVP award.
  • 2011 Bryant becomes one of only seven players with 25,000 points, 5,000 rebounds and 5,000 assists.
  • 2012 Bryant scores 27 points to become the leading scorer in NBA All-Star game history.
  • 2012 Bryant surpasses Magic Johnson's LA Lakers record for most steals.
  • 2012 Bryant becomes the youngest player to score 30,000 points aged 34years and 104 days.
  • 2014 Bryant becomes the oldest player to record 30 points, 10 rebounds and 10 assists in a game at age 36.
  • 2014 Bryant becomes the league's third highest ever scorer with 32,292 points surpassing his idol Jordan.
  • 2015 Bryant begins what would be his 20th and last season in the NBA, announcing on November 29th that he would retire at the end of the season, also surpassing John Stockton's record for most seasons with one team.
  • 2016 Bryant scores 60 points in the season finale, his last game, outscoring the entire Utah Jazz team 23-21 in the 4th quarter and leading the Lakers to a 101-96 victory. In doing so, Bryant become the oldest NBA player to score 60 or more points at 37 years and 234 days old. Despite his final game heroics the Lakers finished the season with a franchise worst 17-65 record.
